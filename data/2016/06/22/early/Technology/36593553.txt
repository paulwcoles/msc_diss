 Japanese stocks fell on Wednesday despite gains on the US markets following a testimony by the US Federal Reserve's Janet Yellen.  The head of the US central bank said the US economy faced uncertainty, but that a recession was unlikely. The region's second largest market, Hong Kong's Hang Seng, closed 0.6% higher at 20,795.12. In South Korea, the Kospi index rose by 0.4% to finish at 1,992.59. Australia's ASX 200 ended the day flat at 5,270.90.
