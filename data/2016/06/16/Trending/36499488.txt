 During the holy month of Ramadan, discipline over food is very much on the minds of those observing the fast. Which is why there was a passionate reaction to a Facebook post on the subject, by a leading Islamic body in Egypt. It's been shared over 5,000 times and  liked over 10,000. And there was a lot of anger. "According to the Islamic law, it is very detestable when Muslims eat publicly in Ramadan because this discourages those who fast…but those who belong to a different religion have all the right to publicly drink and eat …" was one comment. Blog by Abdirahim Saeed and additional reporting from Alma Hassoun
